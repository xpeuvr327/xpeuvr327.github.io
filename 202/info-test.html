Introduction

Frank Herbert's Dune is a landmark of science fiction, weaving a complex narrative of politics, religion, ecology, and human evolution. Set in a distant future amidst a feudal interstellar society, the story centers on the desert planet Arrakis, the sole source of the most valuable substance in the universe: the "spice" melange.
